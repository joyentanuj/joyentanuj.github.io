A curated collection of textbooks, problem sets, and online resources I recommend to my students across all levels.

Competition Mathematics

Books for Olympiads

AMC / AIME
The Art of Problem Solving Vol. 1 & 2
Richard Rusczyk
The gold standard for competition math preparation. Covers all foundational topics with challenging problems.
AMC / AIME
Introduction to Counting & Probability
David Patrick
Essential combinatorics and probability for AMC and beyond. Clear explanations with well-graded problems.
Olympiad
Problems from the Book
Titu Andreescu & Gabriel Dospinescu
A treasure trove of beautiful olympiad problems with elegant solutions across all topics.
Olympiad
How to Solve It
George Pólya
A timeless guide to mathematical problem-solving strategy. Essential reading for every serious student.
Number Theory
An Introduction to the Theory of Numbers
Niven, Zuckerman & Montgomery
Comprehensive number theory text covering divisibility, congruences, and Diophantine equations.
Combinatorics
A Walk Through Combinatorics
Miklós Bóna
Accessible yet rigorous introduction to combinatorics, ideal for olympiad and undergraduate students.
Undergraduate Mathematics

University Textbooks

Graph Theory
Graph Theory
Reinhard Diestel
The definitive graduate-level graph theory text. Rigorous, comprehensive, and freely available online.
Graph Theory
Graph Theory with Applications
Narsingh Deo
Ideal for engineering and CS students. Covers trees, paths, flows, and applications thoroughly.
Linear Algebra
Linear Algebra Done Right
Sheldon Axler
A beautifully written approach to linear algebra that emphasises understanding over computation.
Abstract Algebra
Abstract Algebra
Dummit & Foote
The comprehensive reference for groups, rings, fields, and modules. A must-have for any serious student.
Real Analysis
Principles of Mathematical Analysis
Walter Rudin
Known as "Baby Rudin" — the classic rigorous introduction to real analysis.
Probability
Introduction to Probability
Blitzstein & Hwang
Intuitive and rigorous probability theory. Excellent for both self-study and coursework.
